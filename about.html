Hi there!

My name is Brittny Lapierre and I'm a student studying Computer Science at Carleton University.

A little more about me...

I'm a UX enthusiast; I love learning how to make interacting with computers and software a positive experience for everyone. I'm an aspiring full stack developer; my curiousity and eagerness to learn about technology extends to every level of software development.

I have a years worth of professional experience as a Tools Developer Co-op for Ericsson Canada, and I'm currently finishing up the rest of my degree. After gaining the confidence I needed through my co-op position, I have been seeking out new opportunities to develop my skills.

I've obtained a position as a Web Assistant for The School of Journalism and Communication at Carleton University, where I'm helping in the proces of updating and upgrading their web app for apprenticeships.

I met some amazing people durng co-op, like my good friend who taught me about the Carleton Computer Science Society (CCSS). After being inspired from her, I decided to get involved and I am now the head of their Design Team. We work on creative projects for the CCSS such as photography, videography, and web design and development. On my off time, I also volunteer as the head of The Lounge, a place where Computer Science students go to hangout on campus.

I've also decided to try and help students who need guidance by becoming a mentor for The Science Student Success Center. I was given the position of Computer Science Team Co-coordinator along with my mentorship position. As a Co-coordinator for the I help plan workshops and events for Computer Science Students, and as a mentor I give one on one mentoring sessions to any stident in the Faculty of Science who needs encouragement and moral support.

Lastly, I found a unique opportunity as a Peer Helper for the Office of the Vice President through the Peer Helper Program. After menioning my interest in UX and web development, they offered to have me help plan new features for Carleton's 'Current Students' website. What initially was a social media marketing position has now transformed into a role where I get to practice designing user interfaces and get more experience in thinking for the end user.

I chose to join the psychology stream of computer science in order to better understand how to design software for people, and how to best work with others to acheive our goals. I would like to further my knowledge in communications as well, to both express my ideas clearly and to study how people connect with others in these modern times through social media.

I think my passion for Computer Science stems from my love of learning. In our field our tools are constantly evolving and to be as effective as you can be, you must evolve along with them.

If you're interested you can read more about me and my experiences through my blog posts, connect with me on social media, or contact directly.

Thanks!
